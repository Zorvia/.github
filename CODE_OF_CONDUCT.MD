# Zorvia Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we, as contributors and maintainers of Zorvia, pledge to make participation in our community **safe, inclusive, and productive for everyone**.  

We value **respect, transparency, and professionalism**, and we expect all participants to act accordingly.

---

## Expected Behavior

All contributors and participants are expected to:

- Treat **all community members with respect and courtesy**.  
- Engage in **constructive and professional communication**, avoiding hostility, harassment, or offensive language.  
- **Respect differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences**.  
- **Provide clear, actionable feedback** when reviewing code or documentation.  
- **Credit contributions properly** and maintain integrity in collaboration.  

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes, but is not limited to:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind.  
- Offensive comments or derogatory language related to race, gender, sexual orientation, religion, nationality, disability, or any personal characteristic.  
- Threats, trolling, or deliberate disruption of community discussions.  
- Unauthorized use of Zorvia trademarks, logos, or intellectual property.  
- Publishing personal information of others without consent.  

---

## Reporting Guidelines

If you encounter behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:

1. Contact the **Zorvia moderation team** via [contact@zorvia.org] or the designated forum/private channel.  
2. Provide **detailed information** about the incident, including context, participants, and evidence if available.  
3. All reports will be **handled confidentially** and investigated promptly.  

Zorvia maintainers may take actions including, but not limited to:

- Warning or reprimanding the individual.  
- Temporary or permanent suspension from repositories, forums, or chat channels.  
- Escalation to legal authorities in cases of serious misconduct.  

---

## Enforcement

Community maintainers are responsible for enforcing this Code of Conduct.  
Decisions will be made **fairly, transparently, and consistently**, ensuring safety and inclusivity for all participants.  

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All public and private repositories under the Zorvia GitHub organization.  
- Community forums, chat channels, and discussion boards.  
- Any event, meeting, or communication officially associated with Zorvia.
